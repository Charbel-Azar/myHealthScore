Front Desk Patient Guide

A clear, kind talk track for introducing myHealthScore to patients who are worried about breast cancer risk. Keep it simple, ask permission, and invite questions.

Quick Goals

  • Be calm and reassuring; lead with prevention and clarity.
  • Explain what it is, why it helps, how it works, and what happens next.
  • Always ask permission: "Would you like a quick 30‑second overview?"

30‑Second Intro (desk or phone)

If you'd like, there's a simple DNA test called MyHealthScore that shows your personal breast cancer risk—even if you don't have a family history. It's a one‑time, at‑home cheek swab. Results help the doctor tailor your screening and follow‑up. It doesn't replace mammograms; it adds clarity. Would you like a quick overview of how it works?

2‑Minute Explanation — The 4 Ps

1) Purpose

The idea is to understand your personal risk early, so your screening plan fits you.

2) Proof (simple)

Risk isn't about a single gene. It combines many small genetic markers into one clear score.

3) Process

You receive a home kit (cheek swab), send it back, and we analyse your DNA. Your result is a clear score—low, medium, or higher—and then the doctor discusses what's best for you.

4) Payoff

If your risk is higher, we consider earlier or more tailored screening. If it's lower, we can avoid unnecessary tests. It's a one‑time test that gives long‑term clarity.

Quick Q&A

Does it replace mammograms?

No. It doesn't replace mammograms; it helps personalise them.

What if I'm anxious about a 'high‑risk' result?

The goal isn't to cause worry—it's to act earlier and smarter if needed.

I don't have family history—do I still need this?

Many women without family history still have different genetic risk. This shows your personal risk.

Is it painful or complicated?

No—just a simple at‑home cheek swab.

What about privacy?

Your data is protected and used only for medical analysis and prevention planning.

Cost and timing?

I can share the price range and confirm details before you start.

Objection Handling — One‑liners

"I already do mammograms." — Great—this adds a personalised layer so timing and frequency fit you.
"I'm 30—too early?" — Risk clarity helps at any age—it guides when to start and how often.
"I'm scared of what I'll find." — Totally understandable. Knowing earlier usually means simpler, more effective steps.
