Age‑by‑Age Guide (talk to your clinician)

Guidelines differ by country and personal risk. These cards help you start the right conversation this month.

Ages 20–39

  • Know your normal. Report changes promptly.
  • Clinical breast exam timing per doctor.
  • Discuss family history and personal risk.

Ages 40–49

  • Ask when to start mammograms based on your risk profile.
  • Keep a yearly reminder once you start.
  • Consider dense breast discussion, ultrasound or MRI as advised.

Ages 50+

  • Stick to your imaging schedule per guideline and doctor.
  • Bring prior reports to visits for comparison.
  • Stay consistent, even if you feel well.

This information is educational and not a diagnosis. Your clinician will personalise timing and modality.

Know Your Normal (see a clinician if you notice)

New lump or thickening

Especially if it feels different from the rest of the breast.

Skin changes

Dimpling, puckering, redness, scaliness, or orange peel texture.

Nipple changes

Inversion, discharge especially bloody, or new crusting.

Change in size or shape

Swelling in part or all of a breast, with or without pain.
